Tom Scholz

Engineer and musical genius

Tom in his recording studio

Here's a time line of Tom's life and career:

  • 1947 - Born in Toledo, Ohio
  • 1965 - Graduates from Ottawa Hills High School
  • 1969 - Starts writing music while attending MIT. Joins the band Freehold, where he meets guitarist Barry Goudreau and drummer Jim Masdea, who later become members of Boston. Receive's bachelor's and...
  • 1970 - master's degrees in Mechanical Engineering (from MIT) and works for Polaroid Corporation as senior product design engineer. Vocalist Brad Delp is added to the musical collective.
  • 1976 - Releases his band's first album (self-titled) which becomes the biggest-selling debut album by any artist up to that time
  • 1979 - Boston's second album, Don't Look Back, is released under pressure from the record company, but Tom is not satisfied with the result
  • 1986 - Extra time is devoted to Third Stage, the band's third release and is certified 4x Platinum featuring hit song "Amanda"
  • 1987 - Sets up the DTS Charitable Foundation in support of animal and children's rights, for which he has raised millions of dollars
  • 1994 - Walk On, Boston's fourth album is released and a few months later is certified Platinum
  • 2002 - Releases Boston's fifth album, Corporate America. Tom remarks: "The thing that made me decide to break with previous albums and include an overtly political song, was when I discovered that for the first time in American history big business owns the news media...The public has been sold a bill of goods about the free market being a panacea for mankind. Turning corporations loose and letting the profit motive run amok is not a prescription for a more livable world."
  • 2007 - Marries second wife Kim Hart. Two months later, former bandmate Brad Delp takes his own life - a tragic shock.
  • 2013 - Awarded the Compassionate Action Award from PETA. Toward the end of the year, Life, Love & Hope is released, Boston's sixth and latest album
  • 2017 - Still touring and rocking as a part of Boston, 40 years later, albeit without any original band members.
